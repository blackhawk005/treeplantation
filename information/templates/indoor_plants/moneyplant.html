MONEY PLANT

SIZE

This vine grows up to 20 m (66 ft) tall, with stems up to 4 cm (2 in) in diameter, climbing by means of aerial roots which adhere to surfaces. The leaves on these trailing stems grow up to 10 cm (4 in) long and are the ones normally seen on this plant when it is cultivated as a potted plant.

LIFE CYCLE

If properly maintained, domestic money trees can last anywhere from 10 to 15 years.

CLIMATE

Indirect sunlight with limited full sun is recommended. Also, avoid putting the plant in an area where winds could damage it. The night temperatures cannot fall below 50 degrees.

SOIL

Although they tolerate poor soils, money plants will grow best in fertile, loamy soil.

WATERING/CARE

The best rule for watering is to stick your finger in the pot up to your first knuckle, he says. If it feels dry, water thoroughly over the surface of the pot until water drains into the saucer under the pot.
