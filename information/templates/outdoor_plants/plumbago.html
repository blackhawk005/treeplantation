PLUMBAGO

Introduction

Plumbago is a genus of 10–20 species of flowering plants in the family Plumbaginaceae, native to warm temperate to tropical regions of the world. Common names include plumbago and leadwort (names which are also shared by the genus Ceratostigma).

Size

Mature Height is 3-4 feet tall, 4-5 feet wide.

Life Cycle

Less than 25 years.

Climate

Will do well in either sun or partial shade. My experience is that plumbago does best with late afternoon shade.

Soil

It will grow in any soil, but will perform best if planted with plenty of compost.

Watering & care

After establishment, water deeply (3/4 inch) twice a month. No fertilizers needed.
Pruning: once in early March and once in late August. Prune ½ of the plant in early spring and ¼ of the plant in late summer.

Uses

Plumbago is used traditionally to treat warts, broken bones and wounds.
It is taken as a snuff for headaches and as an emetic to dispel bad dreams.
A stick of the plant is placed in the thatch of huts to ward off lightning.
