MANGO

SIZE

Mango trees grow to 35–40 m (115–131 ft) tall, with a crown radius of 10 m (33 ft). In deep soil, the taproot descends to a depth of 6 m (20 ft), with profuse, wide-spreading feeder roots and anchor roots penetrating deeply into the soil.

LIFE-CYCLE

Mango trees can remain in production for 40 years or more.

CLIMATE

It generally requires Tropical and Sub-Tropical Climate.

SOIL REQUIREMENTS

Mango grows well on wide variety of soils, such as lateritic, alluvial, sandy loam and sandy.The loamy, alluvial, well-drained, aerated and deep soils (2-2.5 m) rich in organic matter with a pH range of 5.5-7.5 are ideal for mango cultivation.

WATERING/CARE

The water table should be around 3 m

USES

Grafted trees usually produce fruit in 3 to 5 years in dry areas, while seedling trees usually take at least five years to come into bearing.
